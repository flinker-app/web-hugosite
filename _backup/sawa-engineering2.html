Chat-to-Quantities with Flinker Copilot and Excel IFC Viewer

How Sawa Engineering used Flinker Copilot and Excel Viewer for reliable BIM quantity takeoff, cost segmentation, and faster planning decisions.

{{< case-meta >}}

Company

Sawa Engineering is a young engineering firm focused on innovative and data-driven infrastructure projects. The team emphasizes lean processes and digital collaboration to deliver fast and transparent results for complex construction environments.

Challenge

Fast, reliable quantities within tight schedules

The team needed robust quantity takeoff and segmentation of reinforced concrete elements by floors and construction phases — including cost mapping — while ensuring decision-makers had transparent data across multiple design variants.

The process had to minimize tool switching and leverage existing Excel know-how to keep the learning curve low and iteration speed high.

Solution

Excel-centric analysis with model context from Flinker Copilot

Sawa Engineering used a Microsoft 365–native approach combining the Flinker Copilot and the Excel IFC Viewer. Quantity takeoff, cost mapping, and model validation run in one connected workflow—no new software stack and no data exports—so decisions are made faster with clear model context.

How they did it

  1. Connected model parameters to Excel via Flinker Copilot and the IFC Viewer.
  2. Applied rule-based queries to segment reinforced-concrete elements by floor and construction section.
  3. Used Excel filters & formulas for cost mapping and variant comparison.
  4. Validated results in Microsoft 365 with live model context—no third-party tools or exports.

The combined workflow surfaced parameter issues early (e.g., mislabeled walls) and kept iterations fast across three design variants. For this public case, a sample IFC reproduces the exact steps without exposing project data.

Results

  • Reliable evaluation of three variants within three weeks
  • Chosen solution shortened shell construction from ~10 to ~7 months
  • Decision-ready data for all variants
  • Iterations significantly faster than traditional methods

Future

Sawa Engineering plans to extend the workflow to further model disciplines, including MEP and façade elements. The next step is to integrate cost data directly into Power BI for live dashboards and cross-project benchmarking.

Workflows enabled

This setup supports the following workflows inside Microsoft 365:

  • Variant evaluation with decision-ready data
  • Quantity takeoff & segmentation (floors, construction sections)
  • Rule-based Excel queries & filtering
  • Model parameter QA & correction workflow
  • Cost mapping handover to schedule & budgeting
